My Story.

Back in 2020, when a harmful virus put the world on pause, I found myself entering college under unique circumstances. Everything was online, which gave me plenty of time to hone my skills. My love for sketching and fascination with computers led me to pursue a degree in Computer Science & Engineering.

During my college years, I was selected as a Graphic Design Executive at the MU-ACM technical club. This role allowed me to polish my design skills and deepen my interest in the field. I also had the chance to connect with seniors and working professionals, building valuable relationships with my college faculty, who were incredibly supportive.

Leading volunteers as part of my role at MU-ACM enhanced my leadership and communication skills. My proficiency in design earned me positions in other clubs like MIIC and NSS. During this period, I developed a passion for 3D design and started learning Blender, an open-source software. My skills in Blender flourished, and I created numerous artworks.

In 2023, when college resumed in person, I had already established a strong reputation with the faculty. In my third year, I co-founded MUIEEE and served as its vice-president. Organizing orientations, events, and workshops, as well as conducting interviews, was an exhilarating experience. I was also part of the team that organized Convocation '22 and the Alumni Meet, which were filled with fun moments.

At MUIEEE, we successfully hosted our university's first E-Sports event, CampusKombat, during our annual fest, Moonstone. Meanwhile, my coding journey continued as I delved into subjects like Data Structures and Algorithms, Computer Organization and Architecture, Database Management Systems, Compiler Design, C++, Core Java, Cloud Computing, and Cyber Security.

Reflecting on my high school days, I remember computer classes where we would enter the lab barefoot, learn basic HTML, and I would experiment by editing HTML files in MS Word to create visually appealing web pages. This early experience sparked my interest in web development.

During the lockdown, I revisited my web development skills, learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I built the frontend of a blogging website for a friend on Blogger.com and even created a website for one of my faculty's companies. In college, I contributed to the development of various websites, including website for our college.

In my final year, I was offered an internship by my university, a proud moment as it was the first time they extended such an offer. However, I declined and instead pursued a research internship where I discovered web development libraries and frameworks. This new knowledge fueled my interest, leading me to build projects and gain confidence.

Eventually, I landed a job at eHawkers, where I quickly became the design lead for an e-commerce project. Then, I received an offer for a government internship from the District eGovernance Society. Leading a team, I developed a mobile application and admin panel for the Lok Sabha election 2024.

Looking back, this journey has been a blend of creativity, leadership, and continuous learning, shaping me into the professional I am today.
